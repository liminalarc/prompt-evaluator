## Problem
The weighted composite ([[2.9]]) is a **mean** — every scorer contributes proportionally to its
weight and nothing can hard-fail a version. Two capabilities were deliberately left out of 2.9 and
have no home until now:

- **Must-pass / veto gating** — a scorer that, on failure, **zeroes** the composite (or bars a
  version from being backport-eligible) regardless of the other scorers. A weighted mean can let a
  strong judge score paper over a hard safety/format failure; a veto scorer prevents that. 2.9
  ships a mean, not a gate, on purpose — "revisit once weighted means are in use."
- **Weight auto-tuning** — learn the per-scorer weights from human labels ([[2.1]] review queue)
  instead of hand-setting them, so the composite tracks what reviewers actually value.

## Value
As a prompt owner I want a scorer I can mark **must-pass** (so a hard failure can't be averaged away)
and weights that **learn from my review labels**, so the one "overall quality" number and the
backport signal reflect real quality bars, not just a hand-tuned average.

## Scope
**In (when picked up):**
- A per-dataset-scorer **must-pass/veto** flag on `ScorerConfig`; its effect on the composite
  (zero vs exclude-then-bar) and on [[2.9]]'s eligibility/target derivation is a design decision at
  plan-time.
- **Auto-tuned weights** derived from human-review labels ([[2.1]]), with the hand-set weight as the
  prior/override.

**Out:** anything already shipped in [[2.9]] (per-scorer weights, the weighted-mean composite, the
composite trend + change table, the weighted backport signal).

## Acceptance criteria
- [ ] A scorer can be marked must-pass; a must-pass failure gates the composite / eligibility per the
      chosen semantics — proven by tests
- [ ] Weights can be auto-tuned from human labels, with a hand-set override — proven by tests
